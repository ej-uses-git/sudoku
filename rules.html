The Rules of Sudoku

The game of sudoku consists of a 9 by 9 grid of cells, subdivided into rows, columns, and block.
The goal of the game: to fill and complete the grid using the numbers from 1 to 9.
However, several rules restrict the player while doing so.

Rule 1 - Each row must contain the numbers from 1 to 9, without repetitions

Rule 2 - Each column must contain the numbers from 1 to 9, without repetitions

Rule 3 - Each block must contain the numbers from 1 to 9, without repetitions

In other words, each digit from 1 to 9 may only appear once in a given row, column, or block.

Other details to take into consideration:

1. Each puzzle has a unique solution

Each sudoku puzzle generated here at EJ's Sudoku has one, and ONLY one, unique solution.
Therefore, guessing here can be double-checked by pressing the Enter key, which will mark every correct guess as green,
and every incorrect guess as red and changeable, allowing the player to attempt again.
For the puzzle to be completed, Enter must be pressed and every tile (which wasn't filled to begin with) must be green :)

2. Guessing is not required

Every sudoku puzzle here gives the player enough information from the beggining tiles to solve the puzzle logically.
There is no situation in which a player will be required to guess the contents of a cell.
